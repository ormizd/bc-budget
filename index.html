Bi-weekly pay explained
!
26 cheques, not 24
There are 26 bi-weekly pay periods per year. That means 2 months each year you receive 3 cheques instead of 2. This app detects those months automatically.
!
Monthly equivalent formula
Your bi-weekly cheque x 26 / 12 = true monthly income. This is slightly higher than 2 cheques because of those extra 2 annual cheques.
$
3rd cheque strategy
When a 3rd cheque month arrives, treat it as a windfall. The surplus distributor will suggest the best use — typically extra debt payoff or TFSA top-up.
