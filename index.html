Canadian account priority
1
Employer matchfree money
Always contribute enough to get the full employer match first — instant 50–100% return.
2
Emergency fund in TFSA3–6 mo
Use a TFSA HISA (EQ Bank, Oaken). Tax-free interest, withdraw anytime.
3
High-interest debt>7% APR
Canadian credit cards: 19.99–22.99%. Paying these beats most investments.
4
TFSA investing
Max $7,000/yr (2025). Lifetime up to $95,000. XEQT or VEQT ETFs are a simple low-cost choice.
5
RRSP
Best when your tax rate now is higher than in retirement. BC combined rate hits 20.06% at ~$57K income.
6
RESP20% CESG
$208/mo per child captures the full $500/yr federal grant. $7,200 lifetime max per child.
Key rules

Pay yourself first
Auto-transfer to savings on payday before spending anything.

Housing over 40%? Use 60/20/20
Metro Van housing often exceeds 40% of income — 60/20/20 gives more realistic room.
✓
Term life over whole life
20–30yr term is better value for most BC residents. Aim for 10–12x income in coverage.
✓
Guilt-free fun is built in
Your Wants category is intentional — budgets with no room for enjoyment fail.
✓
Review monthly
Life changes. Revisit and adjust every month.
